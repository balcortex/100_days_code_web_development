Day 115 - HTML Tables, Pt. 4

Suppose that you want to combine contiguous cells that have the same value in the following table:

<table>
 ...
 <tr style="background-color: darkgrey;">
  <th></th>
  <th>Mon</th>
  ...
Mon Tues Wed Thurs Fri
09:00 Math Chemistry Science Chemistry Biology
10:00 Math Biology Arts Chemistry Arts
11:00 Break Break Break Break Break
12:00 Arts Biology Science Math Arts

Let's start with the row signaling the break hours. To allow a cell to span multiple columns, use the `colspan` attribute on the `td` element and specify an integer that sets the number of columns the cell will occupy:

<table>
 ...
 <tr>
  <td>11:00</td>
  <td colspan="5">Break</td>
 </tr>
 ...

Now, the break time spans all the columns from Monday through Friday:

Mon Tues Wed Thurs Fri
09:00 Math Chemistry Science Chemistry Biology
10:00 Math Biology Arts Chemistry Arts
11:00 Break
12:00 Arts Biology Science Math Arts

Similarly, to allow a cell to span multiple rows, use the `rowspan` attribute on the `td` tag:

<table>
 ...
 <tr>
  <td>09:00</td>
  <td rowspan="2">Math</td>
  ...

Now, Math on Mondays and Chemistry on Thursdays span two rows each:

Mon Tues Wed Thurs Fri
09:00 Math Chemistry Science Chemistry Biology
10:00 Biology Arts Arts
11:00 Break
12:00 Arts Biology Science Math Arts
