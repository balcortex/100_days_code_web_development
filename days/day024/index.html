Flexbox Growing

The default behavior of the flex-items is to inherit the size properties of their element. For example, by turning the container into a flex-container, the flex-items are set next to each other, wrapping their content, and without growing:

Item 1
Item 2
Item 3

To make the flex-items grow to absorb any extra space in the flex-container, use the flex-grow property with a value of 1. This rule will make all the items grow and have the same width:

Item 1 (grow: 1)
Item 2 (grow: 1)
Item 3 (grow: 1)

Other values different from 1 can be used, too. For example, item 2 below has a flex-grow value of 3, meaning it will grow faster than the rest of the items in the flex-container. The exact ratio of their widths is 1/5, 3/5, and 1/5 (because items 1 and 3 have a flex-grow value of 1, so the total width is divided by the sum of all values, that is 5, and multiplied by the item's value):

Item 1 (grow: 1)
Item 2 (grow: 3)
Item 3 (grow: 1)

You can use decimal values to make that item smaller than the others:

Item 1 (grow: 1)
Item 2 (grow: 0.2)
Item 3 (grow: 1)

Note: in these examples, the items will start growing until space is available. When there's no space to grow, the items will be the same size (that's the beauty of flexbox).
