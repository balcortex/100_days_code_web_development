Fractional units

The most basic way to make our grid items dynamic is using fractional units, also known as fr.

The fr unit is a way of distributing whatever remaining space is left in the grid. For example, suppose we have a four-column grid with a total width of 400px and four grid items each on a column track assigned 1fr as their size. In that case, all grid items should be given one fraction of that 400px of space, which is 100 pixels.

We can also mix fractional units with static units (like px). For example, look at the following code:

.container {
 display: grid;
 grid-template-columns: 50px 1fr 3fr;
 grid-template-rows: 50px 50px;
}

That means the first column will have a fixed width of 50px. In contrast, the remaining space in the container will be filled in the proportion of 1/4 by the second column and 3/4 by the third column (because the total available space is 1fr + 3fr = 4fr):
