Positive and negative margins

Margins can have positive and negative values.

  • Use a positive margin to create space between boxes (push away other elements).
  • Use a negative margin to reduce the distance between boxes (bring the elements closer).

For example, consider the following two HTML elements: the background and border are colored. The first box has a top-margin of 40px, so it has more space at the top than usual; the other margins are 20px on the left (separated from the edge of the page) and 20px on the bottom.

The other box has a top-margin of negative 20px, pushing this box 20 pixels up (bringing the two boxes together). Also, it has a negative margin on the left, which pushes the box to the left, leaving a part of the box outside the visible part of the page.
